How to solve a rubik's cube

Step 1(The White Cross):

  • The White cross is done intuitively.
  • It takes about 20% of the total time for solving the Rubiks cube.
  • here are no algorithms for this step.
  • Having a good way to construct the white cross greatly improves your solve time.

Step 2(F2L):

  • The full form of F2L is First 2 Layers.
  • In F2L, you need to do the first 2 layers together.
  • F2L has 3 variations. They are-;
    1. Begginer's F2L or Intuitive F2L - Can be done intuitively and is easy to learn. But, the method is extremely slow
    2. Intermediate F2L - This method is fast and has all the algorithms needed for every case. But, a few exceptional cases are not dealt with efficiently.
    3. Advanced F2L - The most optimal F2L. This method can be completed in 3 to 5 seconds
  • The number of algorithms are dependant on the type of F2L.
  • There are 4 pairs(of 1 white corner and 1 edge) needed to be done

Step 3(OLL):

  • The full form for OLL is Orienting Last Layer
  • In OLL, you complete the entire yellow face. But you need not solve blue, green, red and orange.
  • OLL has 3 variations. They are-;
    1. 2 look OLL - The slow method. You need to solve the yellow cross with 1 algorithm and then the yellow face with another algorithm
    2. 1 look OLL - The fast method. You can directly solve the yellow face with 1 algorithm.
    3. Hybrid OLL - The method where you use 1 look OLL in some cases and 2 look OLL in others. This is neither fast nor slow.
  • 2 look OLL only requires 10 algorithms while 1 look OLL requires 57 algorithms needed to learn.

Step 4(PLL):

  • The full form for PLL is Permuting Last Layer
  • In PLL, you completely solve the cube.
  • PLL has 3 variations. They are-;
    1. 2 look PLL - The slower method. You need to solve the yellow corners with 1 algorithm and then the yellow edges with another algorithm
    2. 1 look PLL - The faster method. You can directly solve the cube with 1 algorithm.
    3. Hybrid PLL - The method where you use 1 look PLL in some cases and 2 look PLL in others. This is neither fast nor slow.
  • 2 look OLL only requires 6 algorithms while 1 look OLL requires 21 algorithms needed to learn.
  • PLL has far lesser algorithms than OLL.
